Life

Our planet is full of life, whether it be flying in the air, crawling on the ground, or swimming in the waters. There are around 8.6 million (+ or - a million) species of life on Earth, including microscopic life.

The Great White Shark is the largest, and most famous, carnivorous shark. Despite what the "Jaws" movie made it out to be, the Great White, and may other sharks, are not very vicious animals, even lesser so than dolphins. It can get up to nearly 20ft long.


The Saltwater Crocodile is the largest crocodile in the world, and is a personal favorite of mine. They can usually be found in Southeast Asia and can get up to nearly 20ft long.


The Anaconda is the largest snake in the world (seeing a theme here?). It is found in the Amazon Rain Forest and can measure up to 20ft long.


The Tardigrade, better known as the Water Bear, is a microscopic organism that is famous for being able to survive in the most extream conditions possible. They're able to survive several hundered degree heat, a couple degrees shy of absolute zero, which is the point that atoms literally stop moving, and they're even able to survive for very long periods of time.


The Tiger is the largest and heaviest cat in the world. Most of its prey is color blind, seeing orange as green, so the tiger has extream camouflage. It is very fast and agile, and the largest species can measure up to 10ft long.
